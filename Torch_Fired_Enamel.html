Torch Fired Enamel

I learned this technique from a book I purchased online titled “torch-fired enamel jewelry, a workshop in painting with fire” by Barbara Lewis.  I watched a few videos from her website and that’s all it took.  I was off and running, exploring color combinations and cutting random shapes from copper & silver sheets.  Click here to see Barbara Lewis (I think) in a short tutorial.

First heat the metal.

Next, dip into the powdered enamel.

 

“ Enameling is the art of fusing glass to metal. The basic procedure for enameling is to apply the enamel to clean metal, and heat the piece either in a kiln or with a torch to a temperature of 1500 degrees or so, until the glass enamel melts and fuses to the metal. Enamels come in powdered and liquid forms, but powder is more commonly used because it is easier to apply and comes in a wider range of colors. Enamel can be fused to gold, fine silver, copper, and steel.”                        written by CopperHeart @
http://copperheartdesigns.blogspot.com/2008/05/tutorial-intro-to-enameling.html

 

Heat again until the powder fuses with a smooth surface.

Repeat this process at least 3 times and allow the piece to cool in vermiculite.
